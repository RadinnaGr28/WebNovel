How did we get here?

The most significant factor leading to overweight is

STRESS

A very high percent of people with eating disorders tend to hate themselves.
They are not capable of noticing the beauty of the outer world, because they are too
overwhelmed with their inner world. While all kinds of stressing thoughts go around their mind,
in the end they find themselves in the trap of the depression. Their depressive thoughts directly affect
the function of their body and this leads to negative changes in their physical health.

It is no surprise that DESSERTS spelled backwards is STRESSED.

Wake up!

Stress is defined “neither an environmental stimulus, a characteristic of the person, nor a response
but a relationship between demands and the power to deal with them without unreasonable or destructive costs.”

Dopamine or the “happy hormone” is a neurotransmitter. It is produced by our body and plays a major role in nerve cells’
connections. When being happy, dopamine is triggered and the serotonin produces hormones in our body that reduce pain perception.
However, when being in the state of despair or any other negative emotion, the serotonin does not produce those hormones anymore.
Therefore, our body needs a quick recover, because the dopamine cannot do its job properly anymore. When feeling weak, we tend to turn to
the most accessible way to deal with this problem – in our particular case, this is FOOD. When you lost something that gave you satisfaction
and love, you start looking for substitutes like drugs, food, alcohol and etc.

But technically, any other way to increase the happy hormone works in the same way. You just have to find something which gives you the satisfaction
the food does. It might be some sport, favorite hobby, friends, family, sleep, parties, sex and etc. This is the key to defeat eating disorders.

This is what we MUST realize before we take action.
